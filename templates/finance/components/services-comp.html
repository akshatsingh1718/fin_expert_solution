Working Capital (OD / CC)

Overdraft (OD):

Overdraft is a popular facility used by businesses that allows the account holder to withdraw funds beyond their account balance, up to an agreed limit. This helps manage short-term operating expenses smoothly. The interest rates and credit limits are usually determined based on the borrower’s relationship and creditworthiness with the lending institution.

Cash Credit (CC):

Cash Credit is another form of working capital finance where businesses can withdraw money up to a sanctioned limit against the security of inventory or receivables. It helps in maintaining liquidity for daily business operations. Like overdraft, the interest rates and limits depend on the firm’s financial profile and the lender’s terms.
